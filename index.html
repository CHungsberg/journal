Processors

The computer processor is the brain of the computer. It can perform millions of calculations every second and does all of the logic comparisons and math manipulation required. It is also the most important factor in determining how powerful a computer is. There are many manufacturers, but Intel and AMD are the most popular. Unlike human minds, computer processors can only operate with binary signals, represented with 1s and 0s.

Although CPUs can calculate incredibly quickly, they cannot do it all in one thread. They have to break up the task into multiple concurrent threads for maximum efficiency. In terms of improving efficiency, there are 3 options. You can increase the amount of cycles per second, increase the number of channels which can process, or process data more eefficiently.

Processors need to store information too, which is done in the form of its cache. The cache is the highest speed storage which is available to the processor, at the cost of very limited space for information. The first alternative is the RAM, which is higher capacity but slower, and the second alternative is the storage drive, which is meant for long term storage, with a massive capacity at the cost of very slow read/write.
